Why Do You Need to Calculate Your Age?

There are many situations where knowing your exact age is important:

  • Government Documents: Passport, voter ID, and other official forms require exact age.
  • School Admissions: Many schools have age cutoff dates for enrollment.
  • Job Applications: Some jobs have minimum and maximum age requirements.
  • Insurance & Retirement: Age plays a key role in calculating premiums and retirement benefits.
  • Health & Fitness: Your age affects BMI calculations, workout plans, and medical assessments.

How is Age Calculated?

Age is calculated by finding the difference between the date of birth and the current date. The calculation considers:

  • The number of complete years between the two dates
  • The remaining months after complete years
  • The remaining days after complete months
  • Leap years (years with 366 days) are also accounted for

Age Calculation Formula

The basic formula is: Age = Current Date ‚àí Date of Birth. However, the actual calculation must account for varying month lengths (28, 29, 30, or 31 days) and leap years to provide an accurate result.

What is a Leap Year?

A leap year occurs every 4 years and has 366 days instead of 365. The extra day is added to February (29th). A year is a leap year if:

  • It is divisible by 4
  • BUT not divisible by 100, unless also divisible by 400

For example: 2000 and 2024 are leap years, but 1900 is not.

What is a HEIC File?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is an image format developed by the MPEG group and adopted by Apple starting with iOS 11 and macOS High Sierra. It uses the HEIF (High Efficiency Image Format) standard with HEVC compression, producing images that are approximately 50% smaller than JPG files while maintaining the same or better quality.

While HEIC offers superior compression and quality, it's not universally supported across all devices, browsers, and platforms ‚Äî which is why converting HEIC to JPG is often necessary.

HEIC vs JPG: Quick Comparison

  • File Size: HEIC is ~50% smaller than JPG at same quality
  • Quality: HEIC supports 16-bit color; JPG supports 8-bit
  • Transparency: HEIC supports transparency; JPG does not
  • Compatibility: JPG is universal; HEIC requires modern Apple devices
  • Editing: HEIC stores edit history; JPG does not
